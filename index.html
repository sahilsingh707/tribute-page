Elliott Smith

A melancholic singer-songwriter known for his soft voice and emotional lyrics

Biography

Elliott Smith was an American singer-songwriter born in 1969 in Omaha, Nebraska. He gained acclaim for his intimate, melancholic songs and delicate vocal style. Smith released several influential albums, including Either/Or and XO. He died in 2003, leaving behind a legacy of raw, emotional music.

Timeline

    Timeline of Elliott Smith's Life

    • August 6, 1969: Born in Omaha, Nebraska, USA.
    • 1980s: Grew up in Texas; began playing piano and guitar.
    • 1991: Graduated from Hampshire College in Massachusetts.
    • Early 1990s: Formed indie rock band Heatmiser in Portland.
    • 1994: Released solo debut album Roman Candle.
    • 1995: Released second album Elliott Smith.
    • 1997: Released breakthrough album Either/Or.
    • 1998: Nominated for an Academy Award for “Miss Misery” (from *Good Will Hunting* soundtrack).
    • 2000: Released major label album Figure 8.
    • October 21, 2003: Passed away at age 34 in Los Angeles, California.
    • 2004: Posthumous album From a Basement on the Hill was released.
